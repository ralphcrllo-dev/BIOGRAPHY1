A Story of Growth and Discovery

Childhood Story

As a child, I really loved being outdoors with my friends.1 We would gather almost every afternoon to play the popular games of the 90s.2 I remember playing langit-lupa, where we had to jump on higher ground to avoid getting tagged.3 We also played tumbang-preso, hitting the can with our slippers and running around excitedly.4 Another favorite was patintero, where we blocked each other's ways with fast movements.5 Tagu-taguan, or a game of hide-and-seek, was always exciting because we had to find the best hiding spots.6 There was also the game of piko, which involved hopping with balance and laughter on drawn squares.7 These games were more than fun; they taught us teamwork and creativity.8 Joy, energy, and endless afternoons were the contents of my childhood.9 The streets were our playground, and every day felt like an adventure.10 Again, through these games, I learned how to interact with people of different personalities.11 Second, I learned patience when it was not my turn yet.12 And third, determination was vital in insisting that one wins.13 Simple activities shaped my character in ways I did not realize then.14 They gave me enough self-confidence to face any challenge.15 It also instilled in me my love for physical activities.16 Eventually, these games later transformed into real sports.17 I started to play basketball with passion, then I got interested in volleyball.18 These sports built up my foundation for discipline and teamwork.19 Now, looking back, I see how these childhood memories have built me up to who I am today.20

Teenage Years

I have spent my teenage years in Quezon City, studying in private schools.1 I was not alone in school because my cousins and brothers were with me, which made it all the more meaningful.2 That school became special to me because it was where I met all my friends who completed my teenage years.3 I still remember how much we loved to play basketball before the pandemic.4 We traveled to places just to play basketball.5 Sometimes, these games caused fights with other groups we encountered.6 And though there were fights, those moments became special memories.7 They bonded us all together as friends, and their bond remains up to this time.8 Once we get together now, they still talk about those experiences with glee.9 There was even an instance when our group had an outing.10 In that outing, some of my friends argued because of teasing.11 The argument blew into a physical fight.12 We were mad at each other, but we all went home together.13 Although misunderstandings occurred, they reconciled before going to our respective houses.14 That taught us how to forgive.15 It also showed us that real friendship can withstand problems.16 My teenage years were full of laughter, sporty times, and lessons about loyalty.17 Basketball was not just basketball; it was a way of bonding.18 Those friendships I built during those years remain valuable to me.19 My teenage years molded my social life and taught me resilience.20

College Experience

My college experience has been surreal because I meet different kinds of people every day.1 I have classmates who come from various backgrounds, and each one has a unique story.2 Despite the diversity, I have found a group of friends I bond with regularly.3 These are the people I treasure and hope to keep in my life.4 I know that in college, nothing is permanent.5 Even the friends you see every day may eventually leave.6 Some may become irregular students, while others may fail and stop studying.7 That reality makes me value my current friendships even more.8 At the same time, I feel lost in my chosen course.9 This is not my dream course, and I cannot yet see myself as an IT professional.10 I sometimes question whether I am on the right path.11 However, I do not allow these doubts to stop me.12 I remind myself that my loved ones are supporting my education.13 Because of their sacrifices, I continue to study with determination.14 I grab every opportunity to learn new skills related to my course.15 I practice coding and attend lectures even when it feels difficult.16 I try to absorb as much knowledge as I can in every class.17 I also join group activities to improve my teamwork.18 My college life is a journey of self-discovery and a test of patience and responsibility.19 I may not be where I dreamed to be, but I am learning to embrace the path I am on.20
